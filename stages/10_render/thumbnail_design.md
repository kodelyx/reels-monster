Vertical 9:16 portrait poster (1080x1920), a scroll-stopping YouTube / Instagram Reels THUMBNAIL and outro end-card for a Hindi tech-news short.

GOAL: a viewer scrolling their feed must understand the story in HALF A SECOND and feel they have to tap. Loud, high-contrast, cinematic — a premium news-drop poster, not a calm design.

## Composition (3 stacked zones, top→bottom)
1. TITLE ZONE (top ~30%): the headline, big and bold, starting with a safe margin from the very top edge so no platform UI (profile bar, time) ever cuts it. Two lines max.
2. HERO ZONE (center ~45%, the dominant focal point): the single hero visual, large, sharply lit, glowing — the thing the eye lands on.
3. CTA ZONE (bottom ~15%, safe margin from the bottom edge): a rounded pill button, white bold text `FOLLOW FOR MORE`, with small like / comment / share icons beside it.

## Headline
{headline}
- Bold condensed UPPERCASE, heavy weight, tight tracking. Punchy, poster-style.
- Strong drop shadow / dark outline so text stays readable over the busy background.
- Keep every letter inside a safe zone — nothing touching or bleeding off any edge.

## Hero visual (center focal element)
{hero}
- This is topic-specific and MUST be the boldest object in the frame.
- Give it a blue neon rim-glow and cinematic key light so it pops off the dark background.
- Never a generic "AI brain" and never a competitor's logo unless that company IS the story.

## Background & mood
- Deep navy-black tech backdrop: glowing blue circuit lines, faint data-grid, subtle bokeh, soft vignette to push focus to the center.
- Dark, moody, premium, high-contrast. Cinematic color grade. Sharp, crisp, 4k-poster quality.

## Hard rules
- No watermark, no signature, no stray logos, no gibberish text — only the headline and `FOLLOW FOR MORE`.
- All text pixel-perfect and spelled exactly as given. No extra words.
- Keep the whole layout within a title-safe zone (generous margins top and bottom).
- One clear hero, one clear headline — do not clutter with multiple competing subjects.
